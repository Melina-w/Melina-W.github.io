Problem Solving

Problem solving is a key skill we will utilise as a developer. Here are some problem solving techniques to try when facing a challenge:

  • Pseudocode
  • The rubber ducky method
  • Reading error messages
  • Console.logging
  • Googling
  • Trying something
  • Asking your peers for help
  • Asking coaches for help
  • Improving your process with reflection

What did I do when I got blocked on a simple problem?

Problem: I needed to access the property of an object but the problem was that the property was inside a variable and I did not know how to solve it.

The first thing I did was staring at the screen trying to understand the task. After writing the pseudo code, I decided to start trying things to see if they work but I was not successful at it.

Then, I thought the answer might be on the previous tasks, but could not find it either. And I ended up googling it but I found 2 or 3 ways to do it, and my question was which one is better? I googled that question as well, and found a few interesting blog post that helped me make the decision. In the end I succeeded at solving the problem using what I think is the most elegant way.

How did you feel throughout the process?

During the process I felt confident I could solve it but challenged at the same time because I was going through the first issue of many of that day. Also, when I finish the challenge, I was really happy because I was able to control my emotions and I did not have the feeling of throughing the computer out of the window every time my "solutions" did not work.

What did you learn?

I learnt that I am able to learn and process new coding information after practicing and reading a bit about it, I guess after spending a while doing the same I will be able to type the elegant solution straight away but at the moment I do what I can and give my best. I remember when I started foundation, I wanted to learn faster because I felt 5 weeks was a really short time for me to go over all the content.Now, even though I still feel it is a short time, I am extremely happy with the way I am learning and putting things together as I am enjoying process.
