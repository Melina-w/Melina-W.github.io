Reflection utilising my journal findings and the Core learnings.

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

After going through the core learning of foundation, I have learnt many new things about myself and my ego specially the relationship between the parts.

I realised that even though I am patient with others’ learning processes, and I can give advice to them on how to approach certain frustration or difficulties, I am not like that with myself, and I tend to put very high expectations on me. This is, of course, an unhealthy habit and it usually has a terrible effect on my self-esteem leading to feeling depressed and incapable of success. Moreover, when I feel overwhelmed or tired, I don’t usually pay attention to it, thinking that it is a normal thing that comes with any job. However, I have never thought it was because of the lack of planned breaks, short walks around or a chat with someone. Last but not least, I realised I can be more proactive when I plan my days and weeks ahead, leaving time for meals, breaks, friends and duties I may have; instead of going with the flow of day after day.

The core learning gave me the necessary tools to realise and change certain habits that even though weren’t working for me, I couldn’t see them. It also helped me to change many sides of my learning process by learning just in time, using the word “yet” because anything I want to do is possible, doing an introspective view of myself and my best way of learning.

What are the role of values,empathy and self-awareness in learning and programming?

The roles of these abilities is fundamental in both learning and programming as they set the basic foundations to live with other people. Values will help us make ethical decisions; empathy will allow us to put ourselves on other people’s shoes and self-awareness will enable us to recognise our strength and challenges and to speak up if we need help.

As we can see, these concepts are important because they give us the chance to collaborate with others’ learning process, projects and become better human beings who are able to support people when needed.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

What surprised me the most was to learn that we can change many aspect of our lives if we want. With the right information and tools, we can reprogrammed our habits, routines, train our brains, learn new things, become whatever we want, and all we need is the willing to do something and stick to it specially during hard times cause that is the crucial part to achieve our goals.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

The most challenging part for me was to write a blog post telling my strength and challenging part because it was a very personal task. I have done a similar activity but it was from a professional point of view, reflecting on ways I have planned my lessons, interacted with students, things like that. However, I liked the challenge and enjoyed the way, if I haven’t been asked to do this, probably I would have never put time aside to reflect on them.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

I think the reason is because many of the students who enrol to this web development bootcamp course are looking for a career change, as the result you need to make sure everyone has a certain mindset while learning and coding. Moreover, nowadays being able to programme in any language is not the only skill required by companies as most of the tech industry jobs need people to work as a team. Last but not least, as a programming school, you need to know what are our needs, challenges and moment of frustrations, among others in order to provide us the necessary support while learning.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practising programming instead? Justify your answer.

At the beginning it felt weird having to post my thoughts, feeling and learning achievements either on GitHub or blog; but then I kind of like to read everybody’s post on their blogs, and get the feeling that I know them a bit more. I think the time I spent reading the core learning part paid of since I have learnt a lot about the way I can help myself and others to succeed. I even started reading one of the books that was mentioned on the core part because I found it intriguing.
