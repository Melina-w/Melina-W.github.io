My Learning Plan

Long Term Goal.

My long term goal is to become an experience full-stack developer, I would like to be able to get a job where I can perform, grow, learn and contribute with my knowledge and experience to the company’s success. Therefore, my plan as a career pathway is to start as a junior, earn experience and knowledge in the industry and after a few years move forward to become a senior developer.

Soft Skills

To achieve my goal, I will take advantage of my soft skills like communication which will be useful to communicate effectively among the cohort, facilitators and other stakeholders. I really enjoy working as part of a team as well in order to collaborate, learn and successfully deliver projects on time. Last but not least, I am self-managed and motivated, as a self-taught developer, I have to do a lot of research and self-study, reach for help to third-parties in order to understand fully what I am studying. However, I always consider it as an opportunity to grow and learn as a professional.

* What skills (non-technical - human skills) would I like to see developed in myself while at EDA?

The ability to know when I can bother a classmate/ facilitator for help and how to approach them both online and f2f. Being able to prioritised while coding and how to cope with feeling overwhelmed.

Limitations

On other hand, as a human-being, I have some limitations while learning. I love studying in the morning as I feel I am more productive and can concentrate better. However, winter is coming!!! and it will be quite hard for me to get up and sit on the desk or go to campus. Also, I am a bit of a perfectionist so I am working extremely hard to stick to the time-box and avoid hanging around details that are not very important at the moment. Last but not least, I find it difficult to take breaks if I’m too deep into an activity. Usually I remember to take a break when my arm is hacking, somebody calls on my phone or I’m hungry; otherwise, I just go with the flow. To sum up, I need to work harder on this but I am sure that with my perseverance and planning as well as with my classmates’ and facilitators’ help, I will be able to do it.

Managing my workloads

In order to keep my mind and body healthy, I commit myself to save time for running and training and keep my diet varied. In order to keep myself out of eating gluten and diary, at home I plan the meals I want to eat 1 week ahead in order to go to the supermarket and buy only what is necessary. Regarding my learning process, I plan to study everyday including weekends being weekdays the heaviest ones as at weekends I spend some time with my husband. As I mentioned before, I feel more proactive during the morning so I usually get up very early and start working until lunch or the afternoon depending on the day. I always have my lunch ready the evening before so I don’t need to worry about what to eat or spend time cooking.

Asking for help

When in need, I always try to google and find the solution on my own but if there is something specific that I can not find, I ask on slack chats to my cohort or to my facilitator. From the facilitators team what I expect is for them to provide the necessary support I need to foster my brain to think and help me see the problems or solutions I am unable to. I also expect them to recommend and advice to read, watch or listen to any extra- resources they think might be useful for me as part of my learning process and goal.
